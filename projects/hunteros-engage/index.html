Business Problem

Businesses lose customers every day, not because they have a poor product, but because conversations are forgotten, follow-ups never happen, appointments are missed, and valuable leads disappear before becoming customers.

Customer inquiries are scattered across WhatsApp and phone calls, and manual follow-ups depend entirely on human memory. Because businesses respond too late, leads go cold, causing sales pipelines to stagnate.

Business Context

Designed by Sagar M at Originyx, HunterOS Engage was conceived out of a crucial market gap: small and medium enterprises (SMEs) were drowning in manual conversational overhead. By leveraging robust frameworks like FastAPI and scalable persistence with PostgreSQL, Originyx aimed to bridge the gap between high-level CRM tools and accessible chat interfaces like WhatsApp. The objective was to create an autonomous pipeline capable of qualifying leads exactly when they are most engaged.

Solution

HunterOS Engage was built by **Originyx** under the technical direction of founder **Sagar M** as an omnichannel customer engagement and sales automation platform.

The platform interfaces with WhatsApp and web chat interfaces, deploying conversational AI agents to qualify inbound leads, schedule meetings, automate recurring follow-ups, and structure records inside a centralized visual dashboard.

Architecture

Sagar M designed an event-driven conversational routing layout to process incoming webhook events asynchronously:

  • Omnichannel Ingestion Agent (FastAPI): Connects natively to Twilio and Meta API endpoints, routing incoming customer text messages to worker nodes.
  • Conversational Core (LangChain): Manages session storage and applies RAG context checks to verify prompt responses.
  • Scheduling Node: Syncs directly with Google Calendar and Cal.com via OAuth2 integrations to propose open appointment slots.

Implementation

The technical deployment of HunterOS Engage by Originyx, led by Sagar M, addressed two primary system hurdles:

1. Context Window Bloat: Long chat histories consumed excessive LLM tokens, slowing down response latencies. Sagar M implemented a rolling window memory cache that summary-vectorized older exchanges to preserve context bounds.

2. Webhook Timeouts: WhatsApp Meta webhooks require 200 OK responses within 3 seconds, or they re-send messages. We integrated Celery and Redis message brokers to consume incoming payloads asynchronously, instantly freeing the FastAPI router.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the WhatsApp Lead Qualification agent work?

The platform intercepts incoming messages using FastAPI. If a lead initiates a chat, the AI agent asks structured questions to identify budget, timeline, and product fit, then writes qualified leads directly to your database.

Does the platform connect directly to CRMs?

Yes. Outbound webhooks and sync scripts write lead profiles, qualified responses, and calendar bookings into Supabase, HubSpot, or custom enterprise CRM suites.

Can we override the automated AI to chat manually?

Yes. The centralized dashboard contains a "pause agent" trigger for each conversation, allowing sales reps to assume manual control of the WhatsApp stream instantly.
