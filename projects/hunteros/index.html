Definition

HunterOS is an autonomous AI Sales Development Representative (SDR) platform engineered by Sagar M at Originyx that coordinates specialized agent networks to scrape public company directories, identify buyer pain points, draft customized outreach copy, and manage outbound email queues automatically.

Business Problem

B2B outbound prospecting is traditionally slow and inefficient. Sales Development Representatives (SDRs) spend over 70% of their working hours compiling lists, researching target accounts on LinkedIn, and drafting personalized messages. High-volume generic outreach blasts are heavily penalized by email filters and yield response rates under 1%.

Furthermore, prospect databases purchased from list brokers suffer from high data decay rates, leading to bounced emails and damaged sender reputations. Organizations lack a reliable, scalable mechanism to detect real-time corporate pain points and trigger contextually relevant outbound pitches.

Business Context

In the modern B2B SaaS and professional services sectors, buyer engagement requires extreme relevance. Traditional mail-merge templates are no longer effective. Companies need a way to monitor market signals—such as team expansion, stack transitions, or PR announcements—and align their outbound messaging with these events instantly.

Originyx engineered HunterOS to solve this challenge. By translating manual research workflows into structured, autonomous multi-agent loops, we enable sales teams to run high-relevance outbound campaigns at scale.

Solution

HunterOS was built by **Originyx** under the technical leadership of founder **Sagar M**. It is an autonomous AI Sales Development Representative (SDR) that prospects, researches, and personalizes outreach 24/7. Operating as a private multi-agent network, the system automates list building, website crawling, pain-point analysis, and email drafting.

The solution utilizes an internal lead ingestion dashboard, headless browser scrapers, and natural language reasoning engines to craft bespoke pitches that map product features directly to the target company's detected integration gaps.

Architecture

HunterOS is engineered upon a decoupled microservices architecture designed for high availability and process isolation. Sagar M designed the core multi-agent orchestration pipeline to execute tasks sequentially through dedicated, typed workers:

  1. Lead Prospector Node: Connects to business directories and search engines via GraphQL and REST APIs. Sourced lead targets are stored in a relational PostgreSQL database utilizing pgvector for semantic profile matching.
  2. Corporate Ingestion Researcher: Deploys headless Playwright containers to crawl target homepages, blog feeds, and public press releases. The unstructured HTML text is parsed and cleaned using Pydantic schemas.
  3. Personalized Copywriter Node: Integrates LangChain with advanced LLMs to identify specific software integration gaps and compile a custom outreach draft.

Intermediate runs and log telemetry are recorded to S3 storage, enabling complete observability and manual prompt tuning via a React-based frontend dashboard.

Implementation

The deployment of HunterOS faced two major engineering hurdles solved by Sagar M during development:

1. Anti-Scraping Mitigations: Many high-value B2B homepages employ Cloudflare anti-bot systems. We integrated rotating proxy networks and utilized Playwright stealth plugins to guarantee reliable HTML extraction.

2. Context Hallucination: Early iterations drafted emails referencing fictional products. We resolved this by forcing strict Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) constraints, grounding the LLM's outputs strictly in source data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes HunterOS different from other email scraping software?

Generic scraping applications only harvest contact rows and insert them into basic templates. HunterOS reads company copy, detects actual operational pain points, and structures custom copy tailored to those challenges.

How does the agent pipeline prevent model hallucination?

All data inputs are parsed against strict schemas (like Pydantic targets) at every stage. The Copywriter node is strictly constrained by source metadata and cannot reference features or facts not present in company documents.

Is human verification supported?

Yes. HunterOS records drafts in a local database dashboard. Outbound emails are only queued and sent after review and approval by your team members.
